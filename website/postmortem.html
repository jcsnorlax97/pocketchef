Postmortem

Our development of PocketChef was that of biting off more than we could chew. For every iteration of our project, we constantly overestimated the amount of work that we would be to do. This meant that we would have to push stuff back to other iterations. This made it difficult for us because we fell behind on what we wanted to accomplish. We had to choose what features we wanted to keep and what we had to push back or get rid of all together. We didn't really have issues with any part of our program, however the database took much longer than we expected it to. Justin and Beni worked very hard on implementing it and they seem happy with what they got done. We finished most of the UI stuff in iteration 1 so that never really popped up again which was nice; we could just focus on the actual function of the app. John worked on the search functionality and the UI regarding the search for most of the project. Each iteration adding 1 more feature and making it work with the database as it evolved. Derek worked on displaying a recipe once it has been searched along with helping out with the overall design of the app. Jas worked on the settings page and the homepage of the app.

Another thing that we constantly struggled with is procrastination. While we tried to schedule meetings and set early deadlines, we just were unable to follow through on them. Whether there was no time that worked to meet, or as discussed above, we overestimated the amount of work we thought we could do; the last 3 days before the assignment due date were always filled with chaos.

I think if we had the chance to redo this assignment the thing that we change the most is setting HARD deadlines, and early deadlines. We caused ourselves a lot of unnecessary stress trying to panic finish the iteration. The thing that took the most time was, by far, absolutely the database. This was new territory for the people that worked on it and it took a long time. Spanning about 1.5 iterations and around 3000 lines of code we have a persistent working database that holds our recipes. The thing that took the least amount of time (at least compared to what we thought it would take) is searching through the ingredients of a recipe. A large part of why we chose to make this app was because we wanted to do something unique and we had never heard of an app that allows you to find a recipe based on an ingredient. We though that searching the database in this way would take a long time to figure out, not to mention that the database had to be setup to be able to be searched in this way. In the end it was actually fairly easy and straight forward to do, which was a huge surprise and a blessing since we had many other things to do as well. We agree that the project turned out fairly well, and similar to what we had envisioned at the beginning. While we didn't get individual accounts/profiles implemented this was a minor part of what we were looking forwards to. We are pleased with what we did, we have a working app that could actually (and I think some people are planning on) be used in daily life. With a couple more months we could add some more features, like automatically parsing and adding a recipe from a website to the app. This would make the app a lot more desirable to use since it is time consuming to add multiple recipes.

This iterations retrospective would not have been very different from the others that we did. We procrastinated which caused us a bunch of extra stress. So even though we tried to have a goal that we would meet regularly and finish early that has not happened.
